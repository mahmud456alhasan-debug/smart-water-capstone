\begin{abstract}
This case study documents four hydrology experiments as an \textbf{integrated Smart Water Decision Support Pipeline}, with critical AI collaboration (9 documented corrections, 83\% first-pass rate), reproducibility scorecards, manual-vs-AI productivity analysis, and explicit \textbf{threats to validity}. \end{abstract}

\section{Integrated system architecture}
Four experiments form one workflow: weather data $\rightarrow$ rainfall alert $\rightarrow$ SCS-CN runoff $\rightarrow$ reservoir optimization $\rightarrow$ flood inundation mapping, with validation and LaTeX reports at each stage. Optional capstone Streamlit integration wires the same modules.

\section{Threats to validity}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{OpenWeather data:} sparse \texttt{rain} fields; mitigated by demo mode and 50-city batch.
  \item \textbf{SCS-CN assumptions:} lumped CN; mitigated by uncertainty band CN $\in [75,85]$.
  \item \textbf{Reservoir inflows:} simplified 7-day vector; mitigated by Monte Carlo and drought infeasibility.
  \item \textbf{Flood model:} bathtub approximation; documented limitations and seed sensitivity.
  \item \textbf{LLM outputs:} hallucination risk; Swiss Cheese verification; 9 corrections documented.
\end{itemize}

\section{Swiss Cheese verification}
Five layers per experiment: (1) AI output review, (2) \texttt{pytest -v} (88 total), (3) physical rules, (4) validation CLI, (5) LaTeX + terminal evidence.
